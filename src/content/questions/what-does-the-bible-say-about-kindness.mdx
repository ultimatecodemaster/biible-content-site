# What does the Bible say about kindness?

## Introduction

The Bible offers a rich tapestry of teachings on kindness, emphasizing its importance as a core value in the life of believers. Kindness is not merely a good deed or a fleeting action; it is a reflection of God's character and an essential trait for those who follow Christ. This inquiry into what the Bible says about kindness reveals how this virtue permeates both personal relationships and broader community interactions. Understanding kindness within the biblical framework requires looking at various passages that illustrate its significance. From the teachings of Jesus to the letters of the apostles, kindness serves as a unifying theme that underscores the call to live a life that honors God and uplifts others. As we delve into these scriptures, we will discover not only the definition of kindness but also its transformative power and practical applications in our lives.

This exploration will address the historical context of key verses, the teachings they present, and how they resonate with our daily lives. By examining these biblical insights, readers will gain a deeper appreciation for kindness as a divine attribute and a necessary component of Christian living. 

## Key Bible Passages

### Ephesians 4:32

- **Context**: Written by the Apostle Paul to the church in Ephesus, this letter encourages believers to embody their new identity in Christ through practical living.
- **What it teaches**: Ephesians 4:32 states, "Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other, just as in Christ God forgave you." This verse highlights kindness as a fundamental aspect of Christian behavior, tied directly to the forgiveness we receive from God.
- **Relevance**: This passage is relevant as it connects kindness with forgiveness, illustrating that our interactions should mirror the grace we have experienced in our relationship with God.
- **Application**: In practical terms, this encourages believers to actively pursue kindness, recognizing that it fosters a healthy, forgiving community.

### Colossians 3:12

- **Context**: Paul’s letter to the Colossians addresses false teachings and encourages believers to cultivate a character reflective of their faith.
- **What it teaches**: Colossians 3:12 instructs believers to "clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience." Kindness is depicted as an essential garment of Christian character.
- **Relevance**: This verse emphasizes that kindness should be a defining quality in our relationships, particularly within the Christian community, suggesting that our identity in Christ should manifest in how we treat others.
- **Application**: Practically, this means intentionally incorporating kindness into our daily interactions, treating others with respect and care.

### Proverbs 11:17

- **Context**: As part of wisdom literature, Proverbs provides practical guidance for daily living and moral behavior.
- **What it teaches**: Proverbs 11:17 states, "Those who are kind benefit themselves, but the cruel bring ruin on themselves." This highlights the self-rewarding nature of kindness.
- **Relevance**: This proverb illustrates that kindness is not merely for the benefit of others but also enriches the giver's life, reinforcing the idea that kindness has a reciprocal nature.
- **Application**: In everyday life, this encourages individuals to practice kindness, understanding that it enriches their own lives as well as those of others.

### Galatians 5:22-23

- **Context**: Written to address issues of legalism, Paul emphasizes living by the Spirit and the qualities that should emerge from a Spirit-led life.
- **What it teaches**: Galatians 5:22-23 lists kindness as one of the fruits of the Spirit, indicating that true kindness arises from a life empowered by the Holy Spirit.
- **Relevance**: This highlights that kindness is not just a moral obligation but a manifestation of spiritual maturity and divine influence in a believer's life.
- **Application**: Believers are encouraged to seek a deeper relationship with the Holy Spirit, allowing kindness to flow naturally through them in their interactions.

### Luke 6:31

- **Context**: Part of Jesus' Sermon on the Mount, this teaching outlines the ethical standards of the Kingdom of God.
- **What it teaches**: Luke 6:31 states, "Do to others as you would have them do to you," often referred to as the Golden Rule. This underscores the reciprocal nature of kindness.
- **Relevance**: This principle is foundational for understanding how to engage with others, promoting an ethic of kindness that transcends personal feelings and circumstances.
- **Application**: In practical terms, this means treating others with the same kindness and respect we desire for ourselves, establishing a standard for interpersonal relationships.

### 1 Peter 3:8

- **Context**: Written to early Christians facing persecution, this letter encourages unity and mutual support.
- **What it teaches**: 1 Peter 3:8 urges believers to "be like-minded, be sympathetic, love one another, be compassionate and humble." Kindness is portrayed as essential for maintaining unity in the body of Christ.
- **Relevance**: This passage emphasizes that kindness is vital, especially in challenging times, as it fosters a supportive community.
- **Application**: Practically, this encourages believers to cultivate an atmosphere of kindness within their congregations and communities, actively supporting one another.

### Romans 2:4

- **Context**: In his letter to the Romans, Paul discusses the righteousness and mercy of God in the context of both Jewish and Gentile audiences.
- **What it teaches**: Romans 2:4 states, "Or do you show contempt for the riches of his kindness, forbearance and patience, not realizing that God’s kindness is intended to lead you to repentance?" This illustrates God's kindness as a transformative force.
- **Relevance**: This emphasizes that kindness is not just a human trait but a divine characteristic that has the power to lead others to repentance and change.
- **Application**: Believers are encouraged to reflect God's kindness in their lives, recognizing its potential to impact others positively.
