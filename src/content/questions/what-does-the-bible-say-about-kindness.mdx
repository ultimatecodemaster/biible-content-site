# What does the Bible say about kindness?

## Introduction

The Bible speaks extensively about kindness, emphasizing its significance in the life of a believer. Kindness is not merely an act of goodwill; it is a reflection of God's character and a fundamental aspect of Christian living. In a world that often prioritizes self-interest, understanding biblical kindness is crucial for fostering a community grounded in love and compassion. This exploration will uncover the depths of kindness as portrayed in Scripture, revealing its importance not only for our individual lives but also for our collective witness as followers of Christ. 

As we delve into various passages, we will see that kindness is intertwined with forgiveness, compassion, and the fruits of the Spirit. This exploration will equip you with a biblical perspective on kindness and practical ways to embody this virtue in your daily interactions. ## Key Bible Passages

### Ephesians 4:32

- **Context**: The Apostle Paul writes to the church in Ephesus, encouraging believers to live in a manner worthy of their calling. This letter serves to instruct the early church in Christian ethics and community living.
- **What it teaches**: Ephesians 4:32 states, “Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other, just as in Christ God forgave you.” This passage teaches that kindness is closely tied to forgiveness and compassion, urging believers to extend the same grace they have received from God to one another.
- **Relevance**: This verse is central to understanding the relational aspect of kindness within the Christian community. It highlights that kindness is not just a personal virtue but a communal responsibility that strengthens relationships among believers.
- **Application**: Practically, this means actively seeking to forgive others and show compassion, especially in conflicts or misunderstandings within the church community.

### Galatians 5:22-23

- **Context**: In this letter, Paul contrasts the works of the flesh with the fruits of the Spirit, emphasizing the transformative power of the Holy Spirit in the life of a believer.
- **What it teaches**: Galatians 5:22-23 lists kindness as one of the fruits of the Spirit, indicating that true kindness is a natural outflow of a life led by the Holy Spirit. It underscores that kindness is not merely a choice but a spiritual characteristic that develops through our relationship with God.
- **Relevance**: This passage is relevant because it frames kindness as a divine attribute rather than a human effort, encouraging believers to cultivate their spiritual lives to experience this fruit more fully.
- **Application**: To apply this teaching, focus on nurturing your relationship with the Holy Spirit through prayer and study, allowing kindness to manifest in your actions and interactions with others.

### Proverbs 11:17

- **Context**: The Book of Proverbs is a collection of wisdom literature attributed to Solomon, offering guidance on various aspects of life, including relationships and moral conduct.
- **What it teaches**: Proverbs 11:17 states, “Those who are kind benefit themselves, but the cruel bring ruin on themselves.” This verse highlights the reciprocal nature of kindness, suggesting that it not only benefits the recipient but also enriches the giver.
- **Relevance**: This wisdom is significant as it presents kindness as a powerful, beneficial practice that contributes to one's well-being. It encourages individuals to see kindness as a wise choice for a fulfilling life.
- **Application**: Incorporate acts of kindness into your daily routine, recognizing that each act not only blesses others but also enhances your own life experience.

### Colossians 3:12

- **Context**: Paul’s letter to the Colossians addresses both doctrinal and ethical teachings, encouraging believers to reflect their identity in Christ through their behavior.
- **What it teaches**: Colossians 3:12 urges believers to “clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience.” This metaphor of clothing emphasizes that kindness should be an integral part of a believer's character and daily life.
- **Relevance**: This teaching is essential as it positions kindness as a fundamental trait of a follower of Christ, necessary for building a harmonious community among believers.
- **Application**: To embody this, actively choose to demonstrate kindness in various situations, allowing it to shape your interactions with family, friends, and even strangers.

### Luke 6:35

- **Context**: In the Sermon on the Plain, Jesus teaches His disciples about love and ethical conduct, redefining societal norms regarding relationships and kindness.
- **What it teaches**: Luke 6:35 challenges believers to “love your enemies, do good to them, and lend to them without expecting to get anything back.” This radical form of kindness goes beyond conventional generosity, calling for selfless love.
- **Relevance**: This passage is particularly relevant in today's polarized world, where kindness can often be conditional. Jesus' teaching encourages a counter-cultural approach to kindness that transcends personal grievances.
- **Application**: In practice, look for opportunities to show kindness to those who may not reciprocate, fostering a spirit of love that reflects Christ’s teachings.

### 1 Peter 3:8

- **Context**: Peter writes to a group of believers facing trials, urging them to embody Christ-like qualities in their interactions.
- **What it teaches**: 1 Peter 3:8 calls believers to “be like-minded, be sympathetic, love one another, be compassionate and humble.” This verse highlights kindness as a communal virtue essential for harmony among believers.
- **Relevance**: Understanding kindness as a communal expectation can help believers navigate conflicts and challenges, promoting peace within the church.
- **Application**: Encourage a culture of kindness by actively practicing empathy and humility in your community, fostering a supportive environment.
