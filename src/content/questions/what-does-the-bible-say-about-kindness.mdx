# What does the Bible say about kindness?

## Introduction

The Bible places immense importance on the virtue of kindness, weaving it throughout its teachings as an essential aspect of a believer's character. Kindness is not merely a social nicety or an occasional act; it is a fundamental quality that reflects the nature of God and the teachings of Jesus. Understanding what the Bible says about kindness helps us grasp the significance of this virtue in our daily lives and relationships. Explore this topic in depth at [Biible.net](https://biible.net?q=What%20does%20the%20Bible%20say%20about%20kindness%3F).

In a world that often emphasizes self-interest and competition, kindness serves as a powerful counterbalance. It fosters community, strengthens relationships, and embodies the love that Christians are called to show toward one another and even to their adversaries. This article delves into key biblical passages that illuminate the concept of kindness, exploring its implications for our conduct and character.

As we examine these scriptures, we will uncover how kindness is portrayed as not only a desirable trait but also a divine command that should permeate our interactions. By reflecting on these teachings, we can better understand how to cultivate kindness in our lives, enhancing our relationships and growing closer to God.

## Key Bible Passages

### Ephesians 4:32

- **Context**: This letter, authored by the Apostle Paul, addresses the church in Ephesus, providing guidance on how Christians should conduct themselves in their relationships with each other.
- **What it teaches**: Ephesians 4:32 states, “Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other, just as in Christ God forgave you.” This passage emphasizes that kindness is intertwined with compassion and forgiveness, highlighting its importance in Christian behavior.
- **Relevance**: This verse is relevant as it underscores that kindness should be a defining characteristic of believers. It encourages Christians to reflect on the immense kindness they have received from God and to extend that kindness to others.
- **Application**: Practically, this means actively choosing to be kind, even in difficult circumstances, and forgiving those who may have wronged us, thereby fostering a spirit of love and unity within the community.

### Colossians 3:12

- **Context**: In this letter to the Colossian church, Paul instructs believers to embody their new identity in Christ, urging them to reflect His character in their interactions.
- **What it teaches**: Colossians 3:12 reads, “Therefore, as God’s chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness, and patience.” This verse suggests that kindness should be an intentional part of a Christian's character.
- **Relevance**: This passage is significant because it frames kindness as an essential virtue that believers should consciously adopt, reinforcing the idea that kindness is foundational to living harmoniously with others.
- **Application**: To apply this in daily life, individuals can make a conscious effort to “clothe” themselves with kindness, being mindful of how they treat others in their words and actions.

### Proverbs 11:17

- **Context**: Proverbs is a collection of wise sayings traditionally attributed to King Solomon, offering practical guidance for living a moral and successful life.
- **What it teaches**: Proverbs 11:17 states, “Those who are kind benefit themselves, but the cruel bring ruin on themselves.” This proverb illustrates the reciprocal nature of kindness, suggesting that those who practice kindness ultimately create positive outcomes for themselves.
- **Relevance**: This wisdom highlights the intrinsic value of kindness, not just as a moral obligation but as a means of personal benefit and fulfillment.
- **Application**: Recognizing that kindness can lead to personal joy and improved relationships inspires individuals to act kindly, understanding that their actions have the power to enhance their own lives.

### Galatians 5:22-23

- **Context**: In this letter, Paul addresses the Galatian church, emphasizing the importance of living by the Spirit rather than by the flesh.
- **What it teaches**: Galatians 5:22-23 lists kindness as one of the fruits of the Spirit, indicating that it is a natural outcome of a life led by the Holy Spirit. 
- **Relevance**: This passage is crucial as it reveals that true kindness is a divine quality that should manifest in the lives of believers, underscoring that it is not merely a human endeavor but a reflection of God’s character.
- **Application**: Believers can cultivate kindness by seeking a deeper relationship with the Holy Spirit, allowing His influence to shape their actions and attitudes toward others.

### Luke 6:35

- **Context**: This teaching from Jesus is part of His Sermon on the Plain, directed to His followers and emphasizing radical love and kindness.
- **What it teaches**: In Luke 6:35, Jesus instructs, “But love your enemies, do good to them, and lend to them without expecting to get anything back. Then your reward will be great.” This expands the understanding of kindness to include love for one's enemies.
- **Relevance**: This passage challenges conventional notions of kindness, urging believers to extend kindness even to those who do not reciprocate, thus reflecting God's unconditional love.
- **Application**: Practicing kindness toward those who may oppose or harm us can be transformative, allowing believers to embody Christ's love and mercy in a broken world.

### 1 Peter 3:8

- **Context**: Written by the Apostle Peter to Christians facing persecution, this letter encourages them to maintain godly conduct despite their trials.
- **What it teaches**: 1 Peter 3:8 calls for unity and compassion, saying, “Finally, all of you, be like-minded, be sympathetic, love one another, be compassionate and humble.” This reinforces the community aspect of kindness.
- **Relevance**: This verse highlights that kindness is not just an individual virtue but is essential for fostering strong relationships within the church and among believers.
- **Application**: Believers can strengthen their community by actively practicing kindness and compassion, promoting an environment of support and love.
