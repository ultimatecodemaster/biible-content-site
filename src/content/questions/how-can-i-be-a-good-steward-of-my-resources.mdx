# How can I be a good steward of my resources?

Being a good steward of your resources involves recognizing that everything you have is a gift from God and should be managed wisely for His glory. It requires a thoughtful approach to how you use your time, talents, and finances, ensuring that your actions reflect your values and priorities as a follower of Christ. 

## Key passages

- **Matthew 25:14-30** - This passage illustrates the parable of the talents, emphasizing the importance of using one's resources wisely and productively. It conveys the message that God expects us to manage what we have been given to benefit His kingdom.
- **Luke 16:10-12** - In these verses, Jesus states that faithfulness in small matters leads to greater responsibilities, highlighting the principle that how we manage our resources reflects our character and readiness for more significant tasks.
- **1 Peter 4:10** - This verse encourages believers to use their gifts and resources to serve others, emphasizing that stewardship is not just about managing finances but also about contributing to the community and glorifying God.
- **Proverbs 21:20** - This proverb underscores the wisdom of saving and being prudent with resources, suggesting that a good steward is one who plans and prepares rather than squanders what they have.
- **2 Corinthians 9:6-7** - These verses teach about the importance of generosity in giving, indicating that good stewardship involves cheerful and intentional support of God's work and the needs of others.
- **Luke 12:15-21** - In this passage, Jesus warns against greed and the dangers of hoarding wealth. It serves as a reminder that true richness comes from being rich toward God, not merely accumulating material resources.
- **Matthew 6:19-21** - This passage instructs believers to store up treasures in heaven rather than on earth, highlighting the eternal perspective necessary for good stewardship and the importance of prioritizing spiritual over material wealth.
