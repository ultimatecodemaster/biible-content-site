# What are the Beatitudes?

The Beatitudes are a collection of teachings by Jesus that outline the qualities and attitudes of those who are blessed by God. Found primarily in the Sermon on the Mount, these teachings highlight values such as humility, mercy, and righteousness. They serve as a cornerstone for Christian ethics and reflect the heart of Jesus' message to His followers, emphasizing the blessings that come from living in accordance with God's Kingdom.

## Key passages

- **Matthew 5:3-12** - This passage contains the core of the Beatitudes, where Jesus describes the characteristics and attitudes of those who are blessed by God. It highlights the values of humility, mercy, and righteousness, which are central to Christian teaching.
- **Luke 6:20-23** - Similar to Matthew's account, this passage presents the Beatitudes from a different perspective, offering a more concise version that emphasizes the blessings for the poor and the hungry. It provides insight into Jesus' message of hope for the marginalized.
- **James 1:12** - This verse echoes the sentiment of the Beatitudes by emphasizing the blessing for those who persevere under trial, aligning with the idea that God's favor rests on those who endure hardships with faith.
- **Revelation 22:14** - This passage reinforces the theme of blessing found in the Beatitudes by promising blessings to those who wash their robes, symbolizing purity and righteousness, and who can access the tree of life.
- **Matthew 5:10-12** - These verses specifically address the blessing that comes from being persecuted for righteousness' sake, which is one of the core themes of the Beatitudes, encouraging believers to rejoice in their trials.
