# What are the Beatitudes?

The Beatitudes are a collection of teachings by Jesus that articulate the values and attitudes that are blessed by God. Found primarily in the Sermon on the Mount, these statements emphasize humility, mercy, and righteousness, providing a countercultural perspective on what it means to live a blessed life. The Beatitudes serve as a foundational element of Christian teaching, guiding believers in their faith and conduct.

## Key passages

- **Matthew 5:3-12** - This passage contains the complete list of the Beatitudes as taught by Jesus during the Sermon on the Mount. Each Beatitude outlines a characteristic of those who are blessed by God and describes the attitudes and behaviors that reflect the values of the Kingdom of Heaven.

- **Luke 6:20-23** - This passage offers a parallel account of the Beatitudes found in Matthew, emphasizing the blessings pronounced by Jesus. Luke's version also includes additional blessings and woes, providing a broader context of Jesus' teachings about societal values.

- **James 1:12** - This verse reflects the theme of blessing for those who endure trials, which resonates with the essence of the Beatitudes. It emphasizes the reward for perseverance and the spiritual blessings associated with faithfulness.

- **Revelation 1:3** - This passage highlights the blessing that comes to those who read, hear, and keep the words of prophecy. It connects with the Beatitudes' theme of blessings in the Kingdom of God, reinforcing the importance of adhering to divine teachings.

- **Matthew 5:13-16** - While not a Beatitude itself, this passage follows the Beatitudes and elaborates on the role of believers as salt and light in the world. It underscores the practical implications of living out the Beatitudes in everyday life.
