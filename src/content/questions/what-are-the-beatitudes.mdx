# What are the Beatitudes?

The Beatitudes are a collection of teachings by Jesus that articulate the values and characteristics of those who belong to the Kingdom of Heaven. Found primarily in the Sermon on the Mount in Matthew and the Sermon on the Plain in Luke, these blessings highlight the importance of humility, mercy, and righteousness. They serve as a profound guide for Christian living, reflecting the heart of Jesus' message about the nature of true blessedness.

## Key passages

- **Matthew 5:3-12** - This passage contains the core of the Beatitudes, listing the blessings pronounced by Jesus on various groups of people. It highlights the values of humility, mercy, and righteousness that define the character of those in the Kingdom of Heaven.
  
- **Luke 6:20-23** - Luke's account of the Beatitudes parallels Matthew's but includes different emphases, such as the blessings on the poor and the hungry. This version provides a more immediate and social context of Jesus' teachings.
  
- **James 1:12** - While not part of the Beatitudes, this verse reflects the same blessedness for those who persevere under trial, aligning with the themes of reward and suffering present in the Beatitudes.
  
- **Revelation 14:13** - This passage highlights the blessedness of those who die in the Lord, which connects to the eternal rewards promised in the Beatitudes. It underscores the hope and future blessings for believers.
  
- **Psalm 1:1-3** - This Psalm echoes themes of blessing found in the Beatitudes, emphasizing the happiness of those who delight in God's law and avoid the wicked. It sets a foundation for understanding biblical blessedness.
  
- **Isaiah 61:1-3** - This passage speaks of God's blessings on the poor and brokenhearted, which resonates with the Beatitudes' themes of comfort and justice. It foreshadows Jesus' ministry and mission.
